## Background

The 16 section excerpt files (`s01-lexical.md` through
`s16-tooling.md`) at `spec/sections/` served a real purpose
during the agentic-implementation phase: bite-sized spec
excerpts that fit in an agent's context window when
implementing specific packages. That purpose has been served.

Persistent friction from the dual-source structure:

1. **Section numbering mismatch.** Section files were
   numbered `s01-s16`; `bock-spec.md` has §1-§23. The natural
   assumption "`s13-transpilation.md` covers §13" is false
   (it covers §17). Documentation buildout (D2-D5) would
   have to carry a mapping table indefinitely.

2. **Double-update tax.** Recent design-pass changelogs
   touched both `bock-spec.md` and the corresponding section
   files in every case. That double-update was pure overhead
   — same content, two locations, perpetually at risk of
   drift.

3. **Out-of-context content.** Section files duplicated
   content from the spec in less authoritative form. The
   spec amendment process risked one file or the other
   getting forgotten, producing stale or contradictory
   excerpts.

4. **Authority confusion.** Section files acting as a
   parallel surface diluted `bock-spec.md`'s single-source-
   of-truth claim every time a reader had to ask "but does
   the spec excerpt agree?"

---

## Decision

**Consolidate.** Delete `spec/sections/`. `bock-spec.md` is
the sole authoritative spec document.

Three options were considered:

1. Rename section files to match `bock-spec.md` numbering
2. Document the mapping; don't rename
3. Consolidate and deprecate (chosen)

Option 1 creates link-rot in 18 historical changelogs.
Option 2 perpetuates the friction indefinitely with a
mapping table. Option 3 removes the friction permanently.
The triggering condition for Option 3 — "the agentic-
implementation phase has matured enough that section files
are no longer load-bearing" — is now met.
